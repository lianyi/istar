usr & usrcat

ultrafast shape recognition with/without CREDO atom types for ligand-based virtual screening

Jobs

New job

The input to usr is onefold:

  • A query ligand in 3D format. Totally 23,129,083 ligands collected from the All Clean subset of ZINC are to be screened.

The output from usr is onefold:

  • ligands.pdbqt.gz: the top 1000 ligands structurally similar to the query ligand.

Tutorials

How to submit a new job

Open your query ligand locally in the WebGL canvas. The supported file formats are:

  • MOL2. Your mol2 file must contain @<TRIPOS>ATOM and @<TRIPOS>BOND records. Here is a sample file.
  • SDF. Your sdf file must contain atoms and bonds. Here is a sample file.
  • XYZ. Your xyz file must contain atoms. Bonds will be recovered automatically. Here is a sample file.
  • PDB. Your pdb file must contain HETATM and CONECT records. Here is a sample file.
  • PDBQT. Your pdbqt file must contain ATOM records. Bonds will be recovered automatically. Here is a sample file.

The MOL2, SDF, XYZ and PDBQT formats support multiple query ligands.

The WebGL canvas supports the following keyings:

  • Default: rotate
  • Ctrl: translate
  • Shift: zoom in/out
  • Ctrl + shift: slab

Briefly describe your job. The job description is defaulted to your query ligand filename.

Fill in your email to receive notification upon job completion. Fill in any anonymous email should you not want to receive notification.

Submit your job.

Check your job status in the topmost table.

ZINC ligands

23,129,083 ligands are collected from versions 2012-04-26, 2013-01-10 and 2013-12-18 of the All Clean subset of ZINC and converted into PDBQT format. We thank Professor John J. Irwin, the developer and maintainer of ZINC, for granting us permission to use ZINC with three conditions:

  • We shall provide links to http://zinc.docking.org/substance/zincid for top hits so that users can seek for the most current purchasing information at ZINC's official web site.
  • We shall limit the number of top hits for download to 1000 ligands from a single job.
  • We shall update our ligands when ZINC data is updated so that users can benefit from the most current ligand data.
